Legends of Independence

Click on any card to learn more about these extraordinary individuals

Mahatma Gandhi

Father of the Nation (1869-1948)

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi led India's non-violent independence movement against British rule. His philosophy of Satyagraha (truth and non-violence) inspired millions worldwide.

Non-Violence Satyagraha Dandi March

Bhagat Singh

Revolutionary Martyr (1907-1931)

One of the most influential revolutionaries of the Indian independence movement. He was executed at just 23 years old, becoming a symbol of courage and sacrifice.

Revolutionary Martyr Youth Icon

Subhas Chandra Bose

Netaji (1897-1945)

Popularly known as Netaji, he formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) to fight British rule. His famous slogan: "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

INA Azad Hind Netaji

Rani Lakshmi Bai

Queen of Jhansi (1828-1858)

The queen of Jhansi was a leading figure in the Revolt of 1857. She fought bravely against British forces, becoming a symbol of resistance and courage for all Indians.

Warrior Queen 1857 Revolt Jhansi

Bala Gangadhar tilak

Queen of Jhansi (1828-1858)

The queen of Jhansi was a leading figure in the Revolt of 1857. She fought bravely against British forces, becoming a symbol of resistance and courage for all Indians.

Warrior Queen 1857 Revolt Jhansi

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Iron Man of India (1875-1950)

Known as the 'Iron Man of India', he played a crucial role in integrating princely states into the Indian Union. His efforts unified modern India.

Unifier Iron Man Statesman

Jawaharlal Nehru

First Prime Minister (1889-1964)

India's first Prime Minister and a central figure in Indian politics before independence. He was a key leader of the Indian National Congress and a freedom fighter.

Prime Minister Congress Leader

Chandrashekhar Azad

Revolutionary Leader (1906-1931)

A fearless revolutionary who vowed never to be caught alive by the British. He was a mentor to Bhagat Singh and played a key role in the Kakori conspiracy.

Revolutionary HSRA Martyr

Sarojini Naidu

Nightingale of India (1879-1949)

A poet and political activist, she was the first Indian woman to be president of the Indian National Congress and the first woman governor of an Indian state.

Poet Feminist Orator

Historical Timeline

5,000 years of Indian history at a glance

Key Events in Indian History

From ancient civilizations to modern independence

3300-1300 BCE

Indus Valley Civilization

One of the world's oldest urban civilizations flourished in the Indus River basin. Cities like Harappa and Mohenjo-daro had sophisticated urban planning, drainage systems, and trade networks.

1500-500 BCE

Vedic Period

The Vedas were composed during this period. The caste system emerged, and early Hinduism took shape. The Mahabharata and Ramayana epics were written.

322-185 BCE

Maurya Empire

Founded by Chandragupta Maurya, this was the first pan-Indian empire. Ashoka the Great spread Buddhism across Asia after the Kalinga War.

320-550 CE

Gupta Empire - Golden Age

India's Golden Age saw remarkable achievements in mathematics (zero, decimal system), astronomy, medicine, and literature. The Ajanta caves were created.

1206-1526

Delhi Sultanate

Islamic rule was established in India. Qutub Minar was built, and Indo-Islamic architecture emerged. The Bhakti and Sufi movements gained prominence.

1526-1857

Mughal Empire

Founded by Babur, the Mughal Empire reached its zenith under Akbar. Architectural marvels like the Taj Mahal, Red Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri were built.

1674

Coronation of Shivaji Maharaj

Shivaji Maharaj was crowned Chhatrapati at Raigad Fort, establishing the Maratha Empire which would challenge Mughal dominance in India.

1757

Battle of Plassey

The British East India Company defeated Siraj ud-Daulah, marking the beginning of British colonial rule in India that would last for nearly 200 years.

1857

First War of Independence

Also known as the Sepoy Mutiny, this was the first major uprising against British rule. Though unsuccessful, it marked the beginning of the independence movement.

1885

Indian National Congress Founded

A.O. Hume founded the INC, which would become the primary organization leading India's struggle for independence under leaders like Gandhi and Nehru.

1915

Gandhi Returns to India

Mahatma Gandhi returned from South Africa and transformed the independence movement with his philosophy of non-violent resistance (Satyagraha).

1930

Dandi March

Gandhi led the 240-mile Salt March to protest the British salt tax. This act of civil disobedience galvanized millions of Indians to join the freedom struggle.

1942

Quit India Movement

Gandhi launched the 'Do or Die' movement demanding immediate independence. It was the most massive anti-British movement with widespread participation.

1947

India Gains Independence

On August 15, 1947, India became independent from British rule. Jawaharlal Nehru became the first Prime Minister, delivering his famous 'Tryst with Destiny' speech.

1950

India Becomes a Republic

On January 26, 1950, India adopted its Constitution and became a sovereign democratic republic. Dr. Rajendra Prasad became the first President.

Historical Eras

Key periods that shaped India's destiny

Ancient Period

3300 BCE - 500 CE

Indus Valley Civilization, Vedic Period, rise of Buddhism and Jainism, Maurya and Gupta empires. Foundation of Indian philosophy, mathematics, and science.

Medieval Period

500 CE - 1526 CE

Post-Gupta kingdoms, Delhi Sultanate, Vijayanagara Empire, Bhakti movement. Development of regional languages and Indo-Islamic culture.

Early Modern

1526 CE - 1857 CE

Mughal Empire, Maratha Empire, arrival of European powers, British East India Company. Architectural marvels and cultural synthesis.

Modern Period

1857 CE - Present

British Raj, freedom struggle, independence in 1947, formation of the Republic. India's emergence as the world's largest democracy.

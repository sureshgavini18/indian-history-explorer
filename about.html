About India

Discover the diversity and richness of the world's largest democracy

National Symbols

The symbols that represent the spirit of India

🇮🇳

National Flag

The Tiranga consists of three horizontal stripes - saffron (courage), white (peace), and green (prosperity), with the Ashoka Chakra in the center representing the eternal wheel of law.

🦁

National Emblem

The Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath, featuring four lions standing back to back, symbolizing power, courage, pride, and confidence. The motto "Satyameva Jayate" means "Truth Alone Triumphs."

🌺

National Flower

The Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera) symbolizes purity, spirituality, and unity. It rises pure and beautiful from muddy waters, representing the soul's journey towards enlightenment.

🦚

National Bird

The Indian Peacock (Pavo cristatus) with its colorful plumage represents grace, pride, and beauty. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and holds a special place in Indian mythology.

🐅

National Animal

The Royal Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) symbolizes strength, agility, and power. India is home to over 70% of the world's wild tiger population.

🥭

National Fruit

The Mango (Mangifera indica) is known as the "King of Fruits." India is the largest producer of mangoes in the world, with hundreds of varieties cultivated across the country.

Geography

The diverse landscape of India

🏔️

The Himalayas

The world's highest mountain range forms India's northern border. Home to the world's highest peaks including Mount Everest and Kanchenjunga. The Himalayas are the source of major rivers like the Ganges, Indus, and Brahmaputra.

🌾

The Indo-Gangetic Plain

One of the most fertile regions in the world, formed by the Ganges, Indus, and Brahmaputra river systems. This plain has been the cradle of Indian civilization and supports a large portion of India's population.

🏜️

The Thar Desert

Also known as the Great Indian Desert, it forms a natural boundary between India and Pakistan. Despite its arid conditions, it supports unique flora, fauna, and vibrant desert cultures.

🌴

The Deccan Plateau

A large plateau covering most of South India, bordered by the Western and Eastern Ghats. Rich in minerals and home to diverse cultures, languages, and architectural traditions.

🏝️

Coastal Regions

India has over 7,500 km of coastline along the Arabian Sea, Indian Ocean, and Bay of Bengal. The coastal regions have been centers of maritime trade and cultural exchange for millennia.

🌿

Islands

India has two major island groups - the Andaman and Nicobar Islands in the Bay of Bengal, and the Lakshadweep Islands in the Arabian Sea. These islands are rich in biodiversity and unique cultures.

Languages of India

A linguistic tapestry like no other

🗣️

Official Languages

India has 22 scheduled languages and hundreds of dialects. Hindi and English are the official languages at the national level. Each state has its own official language(s).

📜

Classical Languages

Six languages have classical status: Tamil (declared 2004), Sanskrit (2005), Telugu (2008), Kannada (2008), Malayalam (2013), and Odia (2014). These have rich literary traditions spanning millennia.

🌍

Language Families

Indian languages belong to four major families: Indo-Aryan (Hindi, Bengali, Marathi), Dravidian (Tamil, Telugu, Kannada), Austroasiatic (Santhali, Mundari), and Tibeto-Burman (Manipuri, Bodo).

📚

Sanskrit

One of the oldest languages in the world, Sanskrit is the liturgical language of Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. It has influenced many Indian and European languages and is considered the mother of many modern Indian languages.

Culture & Heritage

The soul of India lies in its diverse traditions

🎭

Classical Arts

India has eight classical dance forms: Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Kathakali, Kuchipudi, Odissi, Manipuri, Mohiniyattam, and Sattriya. Classical music includes Hindustani (North Indian) and Carnatic (South Indian) traditions.

🧘

Yoga & Spirituality

Originating in ancient India, yoga is now practiced worldwide. India is the birthplace of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. The country is home to countless spiritual traditions and practices.

🎨

Art & Crafts

India has rich traditions of painting (Madhubani, Warli, Tanjore), textiles (Banarasi silk, Kanjeevaram, Pashmina), pottery, jewelry making, and handicrafts that vary by region and community.

🍛

Cuisine

Indian cuisine is as diverse as its culture, with each region having distinct flavors and specialties. From North Indian curries to South Indian dosas, from Bengali sweets to Gujarati thalis - the variety is endless.

🎉

Festivals

India celebrates numerous festivals throughout the year. Diwali (Festival of Lights), Holi (Festival of Colors), Eid, Christmas, Guru Nanak Jayanti, Pongal, and many more reflect the country's religious and cultural diversity.

👘

Traditional Clothing

Traditional Indian clothing varies by region. Sari for women and dhoti/kurta for men are common. Each state has its own distinctive style of dress, reflecting local culture, climate, and traditions.
